Next-Gen Gaming is on the Horizon

Every few years a new generation of game consoles makemselves known to gamers across the globe. Eventhough there have been a few hiccups along the way, each iteration has been a huge step up from the last. This week has been an exciting one, considering both Sony and Microsoft have shared some information about the PS5 and Xbox Series X

The Playstation 5

Sony seems to have had the upper hand over Xbox in the 4th generation, partly due to missteps by Microsoft, but mainly because of the incredible 1st party exclusives e.g. The Last of Us, Horizon: Zero Dawn, Uncharted 4: A Thieves End and God of War.
On the 18th, Mark Cerny held a press conference talking about what's to come, and to many people's disapointment, the console wasn't unveiled. Mark instead decided to focus on the sheer speed of the console, transitioning from a HDD to an SSD, which was a delight to many developers due to the freedom that they now have to create boundles universes.

Xbox Series X

In the past few days, a lot of information has been released about the Xbox Series X. And they've of course shown the world what it'll look like, unlike Sony. They've also showcased a handful of 1st party launch titles which has excited many.
Fan favourites like Gears of War and Halo will be makikg a return, as well s new IP's. Gamers seem to have favoured Xbox's announcements so far, but we'll have to wait and see it they can keep up the momentum.
